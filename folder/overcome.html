Overcoming Challenges: The Crucible of Commitment

Chapter 29: Marital Challenges and Solutions

Chapter 29 of "Successful Marriages and Families" by Lloyd D. Newell and the guidance from S. Brent Scharman's October 2017 article "Resolving Conflict in Your Marriage," we can construct approaches to three marital challenges: financial disagreements, childcare issues, and daily stress.

Financial Disagreements

Newell's discussion about the importance of commitment and shared values can be applied to financial disagreements. He advocates for a doctrinal summary that emphasizes a united approach to family life (Newell, 2012). Scharman suggests that love should be at the heart of resolving such conflicts and that contention is not of God. Couples might find it beneficial to have open and honest discussions about their financial goals and expectations, seeking to understand each other's perspectives and finding compromises that are mutually satisfying (Scharman, 2017).

Childcare Issues

Childcare can often place a strain on marriage due to the unequal distribution of responsibilities. Newell underlines the need for individual and family guidance based on the teachings of the Church (Newell, 2012). Scharman points out the importance of approaching conflicts with a desire to understand and support each other. Developing a support network and sharing childcare duties equitably can help alleviate the stress and foster a loving atmosphere for children and spouses alike (Scharman, 2017).

Daily Stress

Daily stressors can impact a marriage negatively when they lead to spillover effects. Newell's work suggests that living by the precepts of the Church, such as patience and forgiveness, can change our perspective and approach to family life, which can help mitigate the effects of daily stress (Newell, 2012). Scharman offers practical advice, such as waiting out chemical reactions of anger and finding productive distractions. This aligns with the idea of adopting strategies that lead to a calm and respectful resolution of conflicts (Scharman, 2017).

Making Your Marriage Successful - Elder Stone

Elder O. Leslie Stone's insights on overcoming marital challenges are deeply rooted in the ideals of cooperation and adherence to divine guidance. He believed that the cornerstone of a successful marriage lies in the temple, where the relationship is not only legally recognized but also spiritually ordained. He emphasized that facing challenges should be seen as opportunities for growth rather than obstacles, suggesting that through these experiences, couples develop character and learn to conquer adversity. Stone advocated for the harmonious balance of domestic responsibilities, stressing that both partners should work towards the other's happiness and well-being, fostering a spirit of kindness and patience. He also highlighted the critical role of effective and gentle communication in resolving conflicts, advising couples to discuss their problems calmly and to avoid contention, aligning with scriptural teachings that associate peace with the absence of contention.

Furthermore, Elder Stone offered practical advice on financial management, encouraging couples to live within their means, avoid debt, and prioritize saving and tithing—a practice he assured would invite divine blessings and financial stability. In terms of nurturing the family, he reminded parents of their sacred duty to teach and guide their children, reinforcing that no success can compensate for failure in the home. In all things, Stone counseled couples to let love radiate, to practice family prayers, engage in scripture study, and stay closely connected to the Church. By doing so, he assured that marriages could not only be successful but also become a source of profound joy and eternal companionship (Stone, 1978).
