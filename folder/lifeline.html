Communication: The Lifeline of Commitment

Key Fact: Communication is fundamental to human existence and is central to personal, professional, and social lives. (Family)

  • Communication originates from the Latin words 'Communicare' or 'Communis', meaning to join, unite, share, or have things in common, highlighting its role in creating connections and understanding among individuals (Sharma, 2017).
  • The process of communication is described as an art of creating and sharing ideas, emphasizing the significance of the quality of ideas in making communication effective (Sharma, 2017).
  • Communication is characterized as a two-way, continuous, dynamic, and pervasive process, essential for mutual understanding and achieving goals in various organizational and personal contexts (Sharma, 2017).
  • Effective communication is vital in fulfilling basic human needs, similar to the need to drink, eat, love, and sleep, underscoring its role in ensuring safety, satisfaction, and social bonding (Sharma, 2017).
  • Communication serves as the foundation for management functions such as planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling, highlighting its importance in organizational success (Sharma, 2017).
  • The role of communication in maintaining healthy industrial relations, facilitating leadership, and adapting to external environments, further illustrating its critical role in organizational and societal well-being (Sharma, 2017).
