Communication and Understanding

Forgiveness and Repentance

"Gardner and Greiner (2012) talk about how true faithfulness includes emotional and spiritual loyalty in addition to physical reliability. When these promises are broken, true repentance and open communication create the way for forgiveness (p. 59).
This point of view highlights the close relationship that exists between the act of forgiving and repentance and good communication, showing how essential both steps are to the goal of healing and strengthening the marriage.

According to Hill, Carroll, and Fellows (2012), family planning and the decision to have children are very personal topics that should be handled with excellent condition, openness, and respect. As couples work through their differences and strive towards togetherness, these discussions might involve forgiveness and repentance (p. 151). This story serves as further evidence that forgiveness and repent are essential to making decisions that are in line with both parties' ideals for settling conflicts.

Enhancing Marital Recreation and Bonding

Improving recreational and bonding time together as a married couple is essential to maintaining a happy marriage since it involves engaging in activities together that promote happiness, intimacy, and understanding. Another aspect of marriage, emphasized by the ideas covered in "Successful Marriages and Families: Proclamation Principles and Research Perspectives," emphasizes the value of setting aside time and effort to engage in things to do as a couple. These activities can include anything from simple home projects to cultural events to outdoor adventures (Hawkins, Dollahite, & Draper, 2012, chapter 5). In addition to offering a break from everyday conflicts, these shared experiences give couples an environment on which to communicate, collaborate together, and enjoy each other's to provide them emotional connection.

In addition, scheduling and attending recreational events on a regular schedule shows how much you value your relationship and highlights the need for deliberate efforts for maintaining your marriage. In order to ensure that both couples feel pleasure and joy in their shared experiences, a commitment to marital time requires flexibility, open communication, and a mutual understanding of each other's interests and desires. According to the ideas provided by Hawkins et al. (2012), couples can improve the quality of their married life by making time for marital bonding and recreation. This will help them to build a strong relationship that is based on respect and happiness shared by both spouses.

Defending and Sharing Family Principles

Hawkins addresses the belief that spouses should embrace and stand up for these principles in society in general, in addition to accepting them for the good of their own family. They saw this effort as a further expression of their shared faith and commitment to one another. He highlights the importance of married couples serving as role models for solid family values in both their local community and outside of it. Couples can strengthen their marriage and provide a good example for others by actively participating in discussions and activities that uphold family values. This strengthens their commitment to these ideas (Hawkins, 2012, Chapter 34).

Moreover, Hawkins supports the idea that a couple's daily lives need to be directed by these ideals. He believes that a more happy and peaceful marriage might result from putting these ideals into practice in daily interactions and decisions. For instance, "The Family: A Proclamation to the World"-derived values like respect for one another, empathy, and open communication are necessary for resolving marital difficulties and disagreements, especially when it comes to issues of faith and convictions. Couples can establish a solid foundation of mutual understanding and support by establishing these shared values as the foundation of their relationship. This is important in creating a strong and lasting marriage (Hawkins, 2012, Chapter 34).
