Communication and Understanding

Forgiveness and Repentance

Worthington (2006) provided great explaination about the steps of forgiveness process and also the in LDS church's own process. Gardner and Greiner (2012) talk about how true faithfulness includes emotional and spiritual loyalty in addition to physical reliability. When these promises are broken, true repentance and open communication create the way for forgiveness (p. 59). This highlights the close relationship that exists between the demonstrating forgiving and repentance and good communication, showing how important both steps to bring healing and strengthening marriage.

Repentance and forgiveness is "an interactive process that strengthens families" and "Two sides of the same coin" (Worthington, 2006). In family planning and the decision to have children are very personal topics that should be handled with openness, and respect. As couples work through their differences towards togetherness, these discussions must involve forgiveness and repentance to serves as further evidence that forgiveness and repent are essential to making decisions that are in line with both of them ideals for settling conflicts.

Enhancing Marital Recreation and Bonding

Improving recreational and bonding in couples is very important of maintaining happy marriage, because it involves in engaging activities that improve their happiness, intimacy, and understanding. Another important thing about completing the engaging activities is to include time and efforts. This acitvities can include anything from simple home projects like outdoors acitivities such as hiking, biking, sea walk, and etc. In addition to offering a break from everyday conflicts, these shared experiences give couples an environment on which to communicate, collaborate together, and enjoy each other's to provide them emotional connection (ch5).

Moreover, committing in attending recreational events and have been scheduling regularly, show how much value or important your relationship are to you. To ensure that both couples feel satisfaction and happy in their shared activity, commitment is needed together with open communication and understanding to each others needs and interests. Couples can improve firm bonds by making time to each other so that they can have a strong relationships based on respect and support each other. Orthner and Mancini (1990), emphasize three types of leisure activities that couples can do: parallel, joint, and independent. Of all of these three activities, researchers found that joint has the highest marital satisfaction in couples and family.
