Strengthening Families

Intimacy

Intimacy in a relationship is when couples have the feeling of being close, emotionally connected, and supporting each other. It means being able to share a whole range of thoughts, feelings, and opinions that they both have to improve their family and contribute to their parenting roles equally. This is also mentioned in the proclamation: intimacy is when two people have a close bond and share feelings for each other. Newell shared the things that stop couples from experiencing intimacy and building strong families. He said, "More than ever, families are under attack. A culture of throwaway relationships; familial apathy and permissive values; and secularism, selfishness, and immorality has made the responsibility to build strong families more challenging" (Nowell, p. 319). Therefore, a culture of throwaway relationships means when something is broken in a relationship or they have problems, they need to throw it away instead of taking time to fix it; familial apathy means a lack of emotions; permissive values mean forcing one another to do something that he or she doesn't agree on; and secularism means having a divorce or splitting up because of a different religion; selfishness means when husbands or wives receive things without receiving any favors in return; and immorality means sexual marriage outside of marriage has made the responsibility to build strong families more challenging and more important.

Elder Richard G. Scott said, “Any sexual intimacy outside of the bonds of marriage—I mean any intentional contact with the sacred, private parts of another's body, with or without clothing—is a sin and is forbidden by God” (in Conference Report, Oct. 1994, 51; or Ensign, Nov. 1994, 38). Elder Scott said that when couples have any sexual feelings or intercourse outside of marriage, it's a sin that God will never forgive or forbid. This is a warning for couples that they need to commit to keeping it.

Passion

When a relationship is just getting started, passions are often something you would love to do constantly. It's defined by both deep feelings of emotion and strong sexual attraction of the things they love to do or seem to be obsessed with, that each of them want to spend as much time with each other. Therefore, "husbands and wives needs to help one another as equal partner and to love and care for each other and for their children" (Nowell, p. 323). Therefore, for me the true passion in a relationship is not just having emotion but a have commitment to mutual support, love, and care each other and their children. As I interviewed my older sister that was married for 3 years now. I told her what are the things that you and your partner can do to bring passion love in your family. She said, shared commitment to uphold and cherish our roles within our family will help them nurturing our love, respecting each other's duties contribution, and create an environment that her family feel happy and everlasting. Nowell highlights the importance of wholesome recreational activities and a culture of love and respect within the family. In families who find true happiness comes from “faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, and whole-some recreational activities” (Newell, 2012, p. 324). Therefore, these are ways that couples need to do to achieve happy marriage and family as mentioned by Newell.

Commitment

Commitment in partnership can make couples happy by committing in supporting each other, treat other with respect, have equal mindset in decision making, show consistency care and love, and spending time together in recreational activity. In the proclamation, it shares how important the proclamation help couples to shapening their relationship stronger. One recreational activity that I like to suggest the couples need to do in their family, by committing to memorize the proclamation. This could help them to remember the guidance of a proclamation not only to them, but their children also. "It took almost a year, but we memorized the entire family proclamation together. Although this might not be possible for every family, it has proven a blessing for ours. . . . Our children have been exposed to activities, debates, and friends that do not support the Lord's view of the family. I am so grateful to know that they have the words of the Lord's chosen servants when needed" ( Johnson, 2009, p. 12). Therefore, having this kind of recreational activity in family will help them and their children to understand the Lord's view of the family, and to share it's teachings to others that don't understand the family proclamation. Nowell gathered some response from the his student about the impact of the family proclamation to them. One of his student name Stephanie shared, "I always keep a proclamation posted by my desk with a picture of my family so I can remember to follow it. . . . Whenever questioned with why I want to be a mom, I can proudly say because that is my divine role. A loving Heavenly Father gave this divine role to me. He will entrust me with his children to raise them in righteousness in the gospel" (Nowell, p.319). Although, Parents need to apply commitment in teaching their children to learn about the family proclamation. So they could build their own happy family when they get married.
