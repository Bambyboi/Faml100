Physical Affection:

    Intimacy in Marriages

    Tenderness and respect—never selfishness—must be the guiding principles in the intimate relationship between husband and wife. —President Howard W. Hunter

  • The scripture 1 Corinthians 7:2-5 emphasizes the role of physical intimacy within the marital relationship to prevent the temptation of fornication. The passage instructs that for a man and woman to avoid sexual immorality, they should enter into the commitment of marriage. It underscores the mutual responsibility of spouses toward each other's physical needs, emphasizing the concept of rendering due benevolence. This implies that both husband and wife have a shared obligation to fulfill each other's physical desires within the bounds of the marital relationship. The scriptural message also highlights the idea that, within marriage, the husband and wife do not have sole ownership of their bodies but share this intimacy exclusively with each other. The temporary withholding of physical intimacy for fasting and prayer is allowed with mutual consent, but the emphasis is on the importance of coming together again to prevent succumbing to the temptations of Satan due to a lack of self-control. In summary, this scripture underscores the significance of physical attraction within the context of a committed marital relationship, portraying it as a means to fulfill each other's needs and strengthen the bond between spouses while guarding against immoral temptations.
  • The concept of physical attraction is intricately interwoven with the teachings on intimacy within the context of marriage, as elucidated in the doctrinal perspectives of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The teachings emphasize that tenderness and respect should be the guiding principles in the intimate relationship between husband and wife, asserting that the sacred powers of procreation are divinely appointed and are to be employed only within the bounds of a lawful marriage. This perspective is rooted in the belief that physical intimacy is not only a means of fulfilling the divine commandment to multiply and replenish the earth but is also an essential component of the love-inspired companionship between a man and a woman, promoting the development of higher faculties and nobler traits of human nature..
  • According to the teachings, the proper exercise of physical intimacy within the marital relationship is viewed as a sanctification of innate desires and sympathies, leading to life, happiness, and exaltation. The doctrine underscores that physical intimacy, when approached within the sacred covenant of marriage, is not merely a plaything or a means of satisfying passions and lusts but is a righteous and divinely approved expression of love. The emphasis extends beyond the physical aspect, highlighting the spiritual and emotional dimensions of love and affection. The teachings stress that true love transcends mere physical attraction, encompassing faith, confidence, understanding, unselfishness, sacrifice, and companionship with common ideals and standards.
  • However, these teachings also emphasize the importance of maintaining the sanctity of physical intimacy within the boundaries set by God. Any sexual intimacy outside the bonds of marriage is considered a violation of divine commandments. The doctrines caution against misusing physical intimacy and underscore the need for cleanliness of thought and action within the intimate relationship between husband and wife. Thus, within the doctrinal framework of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, physical attraction is intricately connected to love and affection, and its proper expression is viewed as a sacred and divinely approved manifestation of marital intimacy.

    What Is Physical Affection Relationship?

    Physical affection is any touch or body language that indicates love and care between two people. It can be as simple as a hug, kiss, or holding hands. Physical affection is important in relationships because it helps to maintain an intimate connection and creates feelings of closeness and trust.

    Types Of Physical Affection And How They Can Be Used

    Physical affection comes in many forms. Some examples of physical affection include hugging, kissing, holding hands, cuddling, massaging, and stroking each other’s hair or face. The type of physical affection you share is up to the two people involved; it can differ for everyone, depending on their preferences and comfort level.

    Discussing Expectations In Physical Affection Between Partners

    To ensure that physical affection is a positive and healthy experience for both partners, it’s important to talk openly about expectations. This includes discussing topics such as frequency of physical contact, types of touch each partner prefers, what boundaries are needed (such as not crossing the other person’s personal space), and how to respect each other’s comfort level.

    What Is The Importance Of Communication When Expressing Love Through A Physical Connection?

    Communication plays an important role in expressing love through physical affection. Expressing your feelings and needs directly is important, without judgment or assumptions. When communicating about physical affection, it’s also important to be open and honest about what you like and don’t like. This will help both partners feel safe and comfortable in expressing their love for one another.

    Creating A Plan For Incorporating Physical Affection Into Your Relationship?

    When creating a plan for incorporating physical affection into your relationship, both parties should discuss their needs and expectations. This will help them set boundaries and ensure that physical affection is being used to benefit the relationship. Once these expectations are established, couples can devise a plan for incorporating physical affection into their relationship. This could include making time for regular physical contact or coming up with ideas for creative and special ways to show love.
