For example, here in Samoa Younger people are expected to defer to their elders and those of higher status than them, such as the Matai. Children are taught from a young age to respect their elders, avoid shaming their family and to sustain certain aspects of tradition. From the age of five, children are expected to play an active role in the family. Samoans will often use the same word to refer to their father and uncles (tamā) as well as for their mother and aunts (tinā). This reflects the close relationship one may have with extended family. Strengthening The Family - LDS >>

This website by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saint. It provides helpful informations about strengthening the family. To view the website click the heading. It covers some the great facts I listed below:

1. Parenting Principles and Practices: Many children experience problems that stem from family breakdown, including depression, anxiety, rebellion, academic failure, social withdrawal, addiction to pornoraphy, immorality, and drug abuse. The Apostle Paul warned that in the last days many will be “disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, without natural affection, . . . lovers of pleasures more than lovers of God” (2 Timothy 3:2-4). President Hinckley stressed the urgent need for effective parenting to address such challenges: “My plea—and I wish I were more eloquent in voicing it—is a plea to save the children. Too many of them walk with pain and fear, in loneliness and despair. Children need sunlight. They need happiness. They need love and nurture. They need kindness and refreshment and affection. Every home, regardless of the cost of the house, can provide an environment of love which will be an environment of salvation.” (Section 1, p. 1-3)

2. Communicating with Love: Christ-like values and communication in love is one of the best ways for parents and children to somewhat know each other and trust build. One should stop bad things - stop saying no more than once; talk only when asked, talk without getting angry at once say how you really feel or what you expect. Children also need conversation - children create good deeds, say good things, praise; bad things to laughter delightful mouthful say how they feel. Parents are kind, know how they feel and should be known, speak without conviction when they have the need to meet their need, speak to their children in love, a way that shows that they know how to be patient. This is another way to raise them then in love and righteousness. (Section 3, p. 9-13)

3. Nurturing Children: "Nurturing children is define as love and care carefully balancing the child physical, emotional, and spritual needs" (Hinckley, p. 15). This will allow parents to help children to overcome challenges in both present and in the future. As the site mentioned that nuturing includes empathy, understand how the children's feelings, and tried to keep in touch with them and find solutions for it. Nurturing parents can help children to figure out the ways to solve their own problems. (Section 4, p. 14-17)

4. Fostering Confidence: Parents must need to fostering confidence in children, which can help childrens to improve their well-being and achievement. When children feels confident, they will experience healthier, happier, and able to manage social and emotional challenges. Building confidence in children, parents need to love and respect and to understand how the children feels like. Therefore, Parents must also need to encourage childrens to have faith in God, so that they will also rely on God's help to solve their daily problems. Moreover, parents can foster confidence through faith, virtue, integrity, and competence by following these principles, teaching children to trust God and themselves. Doing all these will help children to become confident. (Section 5, p. 19-21)

5. Overcoming Anger: As President Gordon B. Hinckley said, "overcoming anger can help family to build stronger relationship and bring peace in family" and "Anger can affect love and affection in family." He emphasize that when we need to overcome anger, we need to pray to our Heavenly Father for help guide us the right path, considering the issues, and start to take action to solve the issues. Also, we need to avoid things that cause us anger, participate in recreational activities, and focusing on spiritual growth. These principles can help us to feel patience, kindness, and great interactions with one another between parent and children. (Section 6, p. 23-26)
