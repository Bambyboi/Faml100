Journey to Joyful Marriage and Family

My Family

My Family key facts about the The family proclamation.

During our Family Home Evening, we delved into an article that holds profound insights into the sacred and foundational aspects of marriage and family life. The article emphasized the divine nature of marriage and the eternal significance of families, drawing insights from "The Family: A Proclamation to the World." Here's a summary of what we discovered as a family:

Becoming Worthy Partners: We reflected on the idea that each individual should work diligently to become worthy partners and mirror the qualities of a disciple of Jesus Christ. We discussed the impact of serious religious commitment on faithfulness and chastity before and during marriage. The Proclamation and Eternal Families: We explored the role of "The Family: A Proclamation to the World" as a guide for understanding our divine roles within the family. It became clear that the proclamation serves as a source of divine principles grounding our relationships in discipleship, covenant making, equal partnership, and the eternal purposes of marriage. Marriage as the Beginning: The family delved into the concept that marriage is not just a legal union but the beginning of an eternal journey. The article highlighted the transformative power of marriage in the context of the Plan of Salvation, where the end is seen as the beginning of an eternal life with our families. Avoiding Marrying a "Jerk": We briefly discussed guidelines from the book "How to Avoid Marrying a Jerk," which identifies behaviors to watch out for in potential partners. Agency and the Decision to Marry: We emphasized the importance of agency in the decision to get married. Our family discussed the idea that marriage is a personal and intentional choice, guided by the desire to be with someone you love. The Plan of Salvation and Marriage: We explored the connection between the Plan of Salvation, the Atonement of Jesus Christ, and the sacred covenant of marriage. It became evident that through the Atonement, families can be eternal. The Pure Love of Christ: We discussed Elder Uchtdorf's counsel about letting the pure love of Christ fill our hearts as a key to solving problems within families and marriages. The importance of charity in relationships was emphasized.
  • Marriage is Ordained of God:

    We were reminded that the proclamation declares marriage between a man and a woman as ordained of God. This divine institution is at the core of the Creator's plan for our eternal destiny. It sparked a meaningful discussion within our family about the role of marriage in both our religious beliefs and the broader societal context.

  • Cleaving and Becoming One Flesh:

    We explored the scriptural concept of "cleaving" from Genesis, understanding the deep unity and oneness that should exist in a marriage. The discussion touched on the significance of physical intimacy as a sacred gift from our Father and its role in nurturing the bonds between spouses.

  • True Love and Sexual Intimacy:

    The authors highlighted the importance of true love in a marriage, emphasizing that physical intimacy goes beyond mere procreation or pleasure; it has deeper spiritual and emotional dimensions. Our family engaged in a candid conversation about the need for open communication on this topic within our family and how it contributes to a healthy marital relationship.

  • Choosing Love in Marriage:

    We pondered on President Monson's counsel to choose a love and love what you choose. The article underscored that physical intimacy is a powerful way to demonstrate and strengthen the love between spouses.

  • Understanding the Dimension of Sexual Intimacy:

    The article pointed out that many couples fail to understand the important dimension of sexual intimacy, reducing it to mere procreation or pleasure. Our family discussed the role of love, knowledge, and covenants in fostering a successful and fulfilling sexual relationship within marriage.
