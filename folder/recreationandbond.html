Enhancing Marital Recreation and Bonding

As I studied through Chapter 5, I learned so many things that spouses need to do to maintain strong relationships. First, engaging in shared recreational activities could help couples have strong communication and connection between them. I also learned from this chapter that regular participation in enjoyable activities together will increase their level of satisfaction, communication, trust, sharing laughter, gaining everlasting memories, and intimacy in their relationship. This will actually strengthen their emotional bond between them (Harkwins et al., 2021).

Moreover, marital recreational activities need efforts and prioritize time. Which can improve communication in any relationship. Gottman (1999) shared that spending much time together will allow couples to increase their interactions with one another, gain much understanding, and openly converse about their feelings and thoughts. In my personal thoughts, applying these to recreational activities such as those that deal with physical interactions will help the couples create opportunities, increase emotional connection, and enhance their marital bonds. One of the important purposes of sexuality or physical interaction in marriage is to express love to each other, “to bind husband and wife together in loyalty, fidelity, consideration, and common purpose” (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, 2004, p. 26).

Here are some of the Examples of recreational activities:

Sharing activities, like any activity that allows couples to interact together and share their laughter, moments of happiness, and fun, This will actually make those shared activities their hobbies and help them spend as much time together as possible to make unforgettable memories. Also, in "Successful Marriages and Families: Proclamation Principles and Research Perspectives by Hawkins," it is emphasized that sharing activities can increase intimacy and clear understanding between partners. In chapter 5, it stated that "M. Holland (1998) explained that one of the benefits of marriage is to obtain peace of mind. He stated that the pleasure derived from sexual intercourse between a husband and wife is an example of happiness in the afterlife." Therefore, having physical activity with your partner, like sexual intercourse or any other related activity, could help husbands and wives increase their sexual intimacy. Additionally, there are some enjoyable activities on the list below that will enable them to demonstrate their interests, satisfaction, and support. "Shared activities can be rewarding and valuable in building a healthy marriage, but when spousal support of individual leisure interests wanes, marital satisfaction may be negatively affected." For example, if your partner doesn't hate spending a lot of money, then you must include that in your notebook to prevent arguments.

Creating Traditions:

Establishing traditions around recreational activities, such as annual vacations or weekly game nights, can provide a sense of stability and anticipation for couples. These traditions become cherished parts of the relationship's history (Doherty, 1997). Some scholars call these activities rituals. Doherty (1997) has suggested three kinds of rituals to help couples: connection rituals (to maintain the bond between two people) like setting time aside for couples validating conversations; love rituals (to keep the romance alive in marriage) like couples wedding anniversary; and celebration rituals (to show honor, love, and respect for each other) like gifting each other on individual birthdays.

Creating Tradition activity

  • Seasonal Decorations: Spend time with your spouse to decorate your home according to the seasons. For example, Christmas season. Couples must use lights to decorate their homes as world-wide do. This could help couples to express their feelings together.
  • Renewal of Vows: Couples must show celebration when it comes to their wedding anniversary, so they could feel that they both value and important to each other.

Orthner and Mancini (1990), emphasize three types of leisure activities that couples can do: parallel, joint, and independent. Of all of these three activities, researchers found that joint has the highest marital satisfaction in couples.



Independent Activities: This is when one individuals engage in leisure acitivities without the involment of one another.

    Examples include:
  • Reading a Book: Curling up with a good book, lost in the world of words and imagination.
  • Running or Jogging: Enjoying the outdoors while getting some exercise.
  • Painting or Drawing: Expressing creativity on canvas or paper.
  • Listening to Music: Relaxing with your favorite tunes.


Parallel Activities: Parallel activities involve family members or friends participating in separate leisure pursuits side by side. While there is shared time and space and minimal interaction.

    Examples include:
  • Watching TV Together: Each person watches their preferred show.
  • Cooking in the Kitchen: Preparing different dishes simultaneously.
  • Working Out at the Gym: Using different exercise machines next to each other.


Joint Activities: Joint activities are collaborative and involve active interaction between one another. These strengthen relationships and create shared experiences.

    Examples include:
  • Playing Board Games: Competing or cooperating with family or friends.
  • Going for a Hike: Exploring nature together.
  • Cooking a Meal Together: Collaborating on a recipe.
    • Dancing: Moving to the rhythm as a couple or group.


As Orthner and Mancini shared that joint activities help couples or family to gain strong relationship and satisfaction in marriage.
