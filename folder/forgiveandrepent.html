In Chapter 20 Repentance and forgiveness

In this chapter 20, I learned so much about forgiveness and repentance from Walton and Hendricks as they shared their opinions. I learned that praying together with your partner every day could improve forgiveness and repentance in them. The impact of prayer can reduce the level of arguments, encourage cooperation, and encourage forgiveness in marriages. "Even in challenging situations, involving God in prayer has been demonstrated to reduce anger, bring peace back, and change relationship goals" (Walton & Hendricks, nd). Holeman emphasizes that "apologies facilitate forgiveness, and forgiveness motivates repentance" (Holeman, 2008). Therefore, couples need to apologize to each other when they have family issues and also pray so God will guide them through problems.

Repentance and forgiveness are "an interactive process that strengthens families" and "two sides of the same coin" (Worthington, 2006). An interactive process means that repentance and forgiveness are not just one-time acts; they need active participation from both offenders and victims, including repentance, where one person acknowledges the mistakes for the improvement of the behavior to prevent any issues, and forgiveness, where the wronged person apologizes and lets go of the mistake and moves on. "Two sides of the same coin" means that repentance and forgiveness are equally crucial in the reconciliation process. As we think of a coin, a coin cannot exist without two sides, and conflicts and healing of relationships cannot occur without both repentance from the offender and forgiveness from the offended. From the Worthington Pyramid model of forgiveness. I learned the steps that each of us and couples need to follow in order to maintain happiness in the family. "As part of the repentance process, acknowledging the crime, showing regret, and making reparations are essential steps that culminate in the victim deciding to forgive" (Worthington & Wade, p. 206). Moreover, these steps will help marriages improve their values, including openness, understanding, and commitment to make a change for the couples to maintain a firm bond.

In Gospel Principles (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, 2009) is emphasis the forgiveness process that members need to follow (Walton & Hendricks, p.205):

  • 1.Recognize the sin: We admit to ourselves that we have done something wrong.

  • 2. Feel sorrow for the sin: Feeling sorrowful, we are humble and submissive before God, and we come to Him with a broken heart and contrite spirit.

  • 3. Forsake the sin. We stop committing the sin and pledge to never do it again.

  • 4. Confess: We should confess all our sins to the Lord. In addition, we must confess serious sins that might affect our standing in the Church to the proper ecclesiastical authority.

  • 5. Make restitution: Insofar as possible, we make right any wrong that we have done.

Worthington provided some steps of how to forgive someone (Worthington, p.206).

  • 1. Recall the hurt: I know that all of us wants to protect ourselves from pain and suffering, so we put an effort to ignore and forget the pain that cause by offense and try to avoid the shame of facing it in public. Therefore, we must acknowledge the issue and be clear about the wrongdoings before we can forgive.

  • 2. Empathize: Empathy is being able to see things from another person's feelings or opinion through their evaluation. It's important to understand their feeling so that they can think of a solution and apply forgiveness. Like evaluating these kind questions is finding the solutions: Was the mistake done on purpose, or was it an innocent mistakes? What were the events that led the offender carry out the crime? Is there an understandable reason for the offender to disagree with the victim regarding the seriousness of the offense? What kind of violence might the offender have experienced in the past? What pain might the offender be experiencing associated with guilt and remorse? Having these in mind will help us to create a better decision of making a solution for the issues.

  • 3. Offer the altruistic gift of forgiveness: When the victim is humbled by an acknowledgment of his or her own behavior and her own awareness, and expresses specific thanks for the times when they were freely forgiven, forgiving with altruism becomes easier. In my group dicussion about repentance and forgiveness. She shared that wrtting her thoughts and feelings in diary will help her to think for a better improvement and establish awareness between the offender and the victim.

  • 4. Commit publicly to forgive: The victim has a better chance of successful forgiveness if he or she verbalizes the forgiveness commitment to another person (for example, telling a friend or counselor about the decision). Some victims have formalized their decision by writing a letter, making a journal entry, or creating a certificate of forgiveness.

  • 5. Hold on to forgiveness: After completing the forgiveness process, victims may still be haunted on occasion by the pain of the offense. During this stage it is important to move forward. When thoughts revert to the painful injury, the victim is reminded that the decision to forgive has already been made. He or she does not have to repeat that process. Also, it is important for the victim to remember that having forgiven, he or she has promised that there will be no paybacks or grudges. Although painful memories are not necessarily replaced by forgiveness, the pain should be a reminder to move forward with one's life instead of revisiting the transgression committed against him or her. Deliberate efforts to stop unwanted thoughts are often unsuccessful. Instead, when victims have successfully reframed their thought processes, it is probably because they have replaced the unwanted thoughts with something more meaningful or important.

Millers Speech about forgiveness and repentance

The importance of forgiveness and repentance in marriage was given by Richard B. Miller, a professor in BYU's School of Family Life. Miller explained how these ideas might improve and mend marital ties in a revolutionary way. He clarified, "Besides a sincere apology, repentance includes striving to forsake our shortcomings and weaknesses. As we continually repent, we constantly try to improve ourselves" (Miller, 2010). This quote by Miller will help couples in their ongoing process of self-improvement and let them let go of previous mistakes for a better relationship, which will cause them to demonstrate repentance and forgiveness toward one another.

Miller shared another important fact about forgiveness in marriage: he said it is a cure for anger, which he mentioned or reffered as a "lethal poison" in harmonious relationships. He stated that "forgiveness is the cure for the poison of resentment." Therefore, forgiveness actually neutralizes resentment. So that it can create space in our hearts for deepening and increasing our love (Miller, 2010). Forgiveness is a deep healing process that increases and strengthens a marriage, rather than getting a divorce.

Using President James E. Faust's teachings as a guide, Miller emphasized the atonement's ability to provide comfort and healing to those who were hurt, in addition to forgiveness for the transgressor, highlighting the atonement's comprehensive ability to rebuild and strengthen marital bonds. He said, "Through the atonement, He lifts from us sorrow and pain, which then gives us the capacity to forgive our spouse when he or she has seriously offended us" (Miller, 2010, p. 2). This provides light on the atonement's unique role in atonement and repentance, providing hope for marriages that are strained due to miscommunication and transgressions.

The importance of Forgiveness and Repentance in Marriages

    Foundation for Healing:

    Forgiveness and repentance are necessary for healing past hurts and confusion in a married partnership and it allows couples to put their disagreements behind them and create a closer, more compassionate bond.

    Promotes Open and positive Communication:

    Having positive communication and open honesty communication about each other's opinion, feelings and expectations is part of a forgiveness. Because it will let them hear the other persons thoughts without blaming. And establishing awareness to one another known as having boundaries. This will increases their empathy and compassion in their relationship.

    Fosters Emotional Intimacy:

    Couples that actively seek and offer forgiveness to one another become closer emotionally and shows trust and vulnerability which are key components of emotional intimacy.

    Reduces Resentment:

    Resentment is toxic to any relationship and can be remindful by holding onto grudges and past mistakes. Getting rid of these hurtful emotions and ensuring they don't remain and create further harm can be demonstrate by forgiving one another through have an open communication.

    Facilitates Emotional Healing:

    By allowing both couples to move past complaints and mistakes, forgiveness reduces resentment and anger, which may let both have a honesty conversation. In my opinion, repentance and forgiveness from one spouse can heal emotional wounds and create a more understanding and supporting one another. This will make easier for both partners to communicate their wants and feelings honestly as a result of this emotional healing process as they are no longer afraid of judgment.

    Builds Trust:

    Repentance may allow both couples to comform each other from past mistakes and allow them to gain much more trust in them. Because trust is increased through mutual exchange and become easier for them to have a open communication if they experience the same issues.
