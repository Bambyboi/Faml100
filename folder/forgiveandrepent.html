  • Resist the desire to rescue an unhappy soul who pours his or her heart out to you.
  • Don't share the most painful things of your soul with an attractive alternative. This develops deep levels of intimacy.
  • If a conversation makes light of marriage, respond with something positive about your own marriage.
  • Discuss marital issues with your spouse. Work on the problems at home. If you do need to talk to someone else about your marriage, be sure he or she is a friend of the marriage.
  • Don't have lunch or take work breaks with the same person all the time.
  • Don't have lunch alone with an old flame.
  • If an old boyfriend or girlfriend is going to be at a class reunion, make sure you bring your spouse along.
  • When you travel with a coworker, meet only in public places.
  • Don't flirt with anyone other than your spouse.
  • Don't travel together with someone of the opposite sex when going to meetings for work, church, or in other circumstances.

Millers Speech about forgiveness and repentance

The important of forgiveness and repentance in marriage was given by Richard B. Miller speech, a professor in BYU's School of Family Life. Miller explained how these ideas might improve and mend marital ties in a revolutionary way. He clarified, "Besides a sincere apology, repentance includes striving to forsake our shortcomings and weaknesses... As we continually repent, we constantly try to improve ourselves" (Miller, 2010). This quotes by Miller will help couples to ongoing process of self-improvement and let them to let go of previous mistakes for better relationship, which will cause as they demonstrate repentance and forgiveness to one another

Miller shared another importance fact about forgiveness in marriage, he said it is cure for anger, which he mentioned or reffered it as a "lethal poison" harmonious relationships. He stated that "Forgiveness is the cure for the poison of resentment." Therefore, resentment is actually neutralized by forgiveness. So than it can create space in our hearts for deepening and increasing our love (Miller, 2010). Forgiveness is a deep healing process to increase and strengthens a marriage, rather than getting a divorce.

Using President James E. Faust's teachings as a guide, Miller emphasized the Atonement's ability to provide comfort and healing to those who were hurt within addition to forgiveness for the transgressor, highlighting the Atonement's comprehensive ability to rebuild and strengthen marital bonds. He said, "Through the Atonement He lifts from us sorrow and pain, which then gives us the capacity to forgive our spouse when he or she has seriously offended us" (Miller, 2010, p. 2). This provides light on the Atonement's unique role in atonement and repentance, providing hope for marriages that are strained due to miscommunication and transgressions.

The importance of Forgiveness and Repentance in Marriages

    Foundation for Healing:

    Forgiveness and repentance are necessary for healing past hurts and confusion in a married partnership and it allows couples to put their disagreements behind them and create a closer, more compassionate bond.

    Promotes Open and positive Communication:

    Having positive communication and open honesty communication about each other's opinion, feelings and expectations is part of a forgiveness. Because it will let them hear the other persons thoughts without blaming. And establishing awareness to one another known as having boundaries. This will increases their empathy and compassion in their relationship.

    Fosters Emotional Intimacy:

    Couples that actively seek and offer forgiveness to one another become closer emotionally and shows trust and vulnerability which are key components of emotional intimacy.

    Reduces Resentment:

    Resentment is toxic to any relationship and can be remindful by holding onto grudges and past mistakes. Getting rid of these hurtful emotions and ensuring they don't remain and create further harm can be demonstrate by forgiving one another through have an open communication.

    Facilitates Emotional Healing:

    By allowing both couples to move past complaints and mistakes, forgiveness reduces resentment and anger, which may let both have a honesty conversation. In my opinion, repentance and forgiveness from one spouse can heal emotional wounds and create a more understanding and supporting one another. This will make easier for both partners to communicate their wants and feelings honestly as a result of this emotional healing process as they are no longer afraid of judgment.

    Builds Trust:

    Repentance may allow both couples to comform each other from past mistakes and allow them to gain much more trust in them. Because trust is increased through mutual exchange and become easier for them to have a open communication if they experience the same issues.
